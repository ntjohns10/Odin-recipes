Whole Grilled Fish

The whole grilled fish is both rustic and beautiful, there are no frills about a whole fish ready to be eaten on a plate. This dish is simple, served with lemon to squeeze over the fish.

For the Fish

  • 1 White Fleshed Fish, Gutted and Scaled (About 2 Pounds)
  • Kosher Salt
  • 3 Bay Leaves
  • 1 Lemon; 1 Half Cut Into Wedges, 1 Half Left Whole
  • Butcher's Twine

Make the Fish

  1. Prepare a medium hot charcoal fire or preheat a gas grill
  2. Season the inside and the outside of the fish with salt
  3. Stuff the fish with the bay leaves and lemon wedges
  4. Tie butcher's twine around the gills and the base of the tail to secure the bay and lemon
  5. Grill the fish 8 minutes per side, until the fish flakes...do not overcook
  6. Remove and discard the butcher's twinevand remove the bay and lemon
  7. Transfer the fish to a large seving platter and squeeze the whole lemon over the fish. Be carefule to catch any lemon seeds.
  8. Serve Immediately
