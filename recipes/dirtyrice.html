Dirty Rice

Dirty rice is a Southern staple. Historically made as a catch all for whatever bits and pieces may be leftover in the fridge, this dish serves as a sort of southern fried rice. Dirty rice can be made with any protein to become a one pot meal. Note that the chicken livers must be soaked overnight, don't forget to plan ahead!

For the Rice

  • 9 Ounces Chicken Liver
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • 1/4 Cup Finely Chopped Yellow Onion
  • 1/4 Cup Finely Chopped Celery
  • 1/4 Cup Finely Chopped Green Bell Pepper
  • 2 Teaspoons Minced Garlic
  • 2 Tablespoons Brandy
  • 2 Teaspoons Gumbo File
  • 4 Cups Cooked Long Grain White rice
  • Kosher Salt
  • Cracked Black Pepper
  • 1/2 Cup Finely Chopped Flat Leaf Parsley

Make the Rice

  1. Soak the chicken livers in the milk in the fridge overnight
  2. Remove the chicken livers from the milk, chop finely and set aside
  3. Melt the butter in a large saute pan over medium heat, add the onion, celery, bell pepper, and garlic. Saute for 3-5 minutes
  4. Add the chicken livers adn saute for 1 minute
  5. Deglaze the pan with the brandy, scraping the bottom of the with a wooden spoon and cooking for 2-3 minutes
  6. Add the gumbo file and fold in the rice
  7. Season the rice wth salt and black pepper to taste, cook while tossing to make sure to heat all the weeay through
  8. When hot and seasoned to your liking transfer the rice to a serving bowl, garnish with parsley and serve immediately
  9. Leftover rice will keep in the refrigerator for 3 days, reheat in a skillet
